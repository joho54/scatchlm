Chapter 3: Introduction

English verb conjugation follows a set of rules that vary depending on the verb type and the subject.

In this chapter, we will explore:

• Present simple tense

• Third-person singular forms

• Common irregular patterns

Understanding these patterns is essential for correct subject-verb agreement in written and spoken English.

Chapter 3: Verb Conjugation

The present tense in English is used to describe habitual actions, general truths, and states of being.

Regular verbs follow a predictable pattern:

I walk → He walks

I study → She studies

Chapter 3: Irregular Verbs

Some verbs do not follow the standard conjugation rules. These are called irregular verbs.

Common examples:

to be → I am, you are, he is

to have → I have, she has

to do → I do, it does
