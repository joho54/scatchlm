Chapter 3: Verb Conjugation

The present tense in English is used to describe habitual actions, general truths, and states of being.

Regular verbs follow a predictable pattern:

I walk → He walks

I study → She studies

Exercise 3.1: Conjugate the following verbs in the present tense.

1. (to run) She _______

2. (to fly) The bird _______

3. (to carry) He _______

4. (to watch) They _______

5. (to go) She _______

Rule: For verbs ending in consonant + y, drop the y and add -ies.

fly → flies, carry → carries, study → studies

Exercise 3.2: Irregular verbs

to be → I am, you are, he is

to have → I have, she has

to do → I do, it does

Exercise 3.1 — Verb Conjugation
Present tense, 3rd person singular
1. (to run) She runs
2. (to fly) The bird flys
3. (to carry) He carrys
4. (to watch) They watches
5. (to go) She goes

Exercise 3.2 — Irregular Verbs
Fill in the correct form
1. She is a teacher.
2. They have two cats.
3. He do his homework every day.
4. It does not matter.
5. We are students at this school.

Exercise 3.3 — Past Simple
Rewrite in past tense
1. I go to school → I went to school
2. She sees the bird → She saw the bird
3. They take the bus → They taked the bus
4. He writes a letter → He wrote a letter
5. We begin at 9am → We began at 9am

Exercise 3.4 — Present Perfect
have/has + past participle
1. I have visited Paris three times.
2. She has growed taller this year.
3. We have lived here for five years.
4. He has never seen snow before.
5. They have already finished the test.

Exercise 3.5 — Modal Verbs
can, could, will, would, shall, should, may, might, must
1. She can swim very well.
2. He must go to the doctor tomorrow.
3. They should study harder for the exam.
4. We could visit the museum if it's open.
5. You may leave the room now.

Exercise 3.6 — Conditionals
If-clauses: Type 0, 1, 2
1. If it rains, I will stay home.
2. If I were you, I would study more.
3. If water reaches 100°C, it boils.
4. If she had time, she will help us.
5. If they arrive early, we can start the meeting.

Notes — Personal Reminders
Review before test
- consonant + y → drop y, add -ies (fly → flies)
- irregular past: go/went, see/saw, take/took
- present perfect: have/has + past participle
- modals: no -s for 3rd person (she can, NOT cans)
- Type 2 conditional: If + past simple, would + base
- "grew" not "growed", "took" not "taked"
